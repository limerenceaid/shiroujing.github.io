Research Experience

My research journey is fueled by a passion for creating innovative solutions through computer science and AI. I have focused on Generative Models, Image and Video Generation using Deep Learning, and Integrating AI with Human Needs, particularly in Human-Computer Interaction.

My fascination with science began at a young age. I was captivated by the "Horrible Science" and "Horrible Maths" book series. During a six-month visit to OSU with my parents, I constructed a toy rocket that could fly 20 meters, igniting my curiosity in practical applications of science. In high school, my interest in computers grew, leading me to participate in informatics competitions where I won a provincial first prize.

As a freshman, I laid a solid foundation in mathematics and physics, which proved invaluable during my summer internship at ZhenTai Intelligence, a pioneering startup in brain-machine rehabilitation robots incubated by Xi'an Jiaotong University's Institute of Mechanical Engineering and Medical Engineering. Here, I developed a keen interest in human-computer interaction, contributing to the development of systems designed to aid human rehabilitation.

Motivated by the real-world problem of lab mates suffering from mild tenosynovitis due to extensive mouse use, I collaborated with Prof. Feng Tian and Prof. Wei Sun in my junior year to design an interactive, game-based solution to alleviate the condition. This project not only resulted in a patent application but also led to our paper being accepted by IPCV.

In my sophomore year, I explored various fields such as lip reading recognition, video forgery detection, and radar image processing. During the summer, I conducted research at Xi'an Jiaotong University under the mentorship of Prof. Haixia Bi. My project focused on classifying Polarimetric SAR Images using diffusion models, achieving a significant improvement in accuracy compared to traditional models. This work led to a publication in IGARSS 2024.

My intrigue in generative models, particularly diffusion models, deepened during my summer research. In my junior year, I collaborated with Prof. Shiguang Shan and Zhenliang He at the Institute of Computing Technology, focusing on diffusion models' creative potential. During my exchange visit to UC Berkeley, I brought my research questions and a strong curiosity about AI to CVPR, where I received guidance and encouragement from many experienced researchers. This experience solidified my determination to pursue further studies in AI.

My exchange visit to UC Berkeley was a transformative experience, where I contributed to AI-driven microscopy algorithm design at the ABC lab, founded by Nobel laureate Eric Betzig. This opportunity to work on cutting-edge technology in a world-renowned lab further honed my research skills and reinforced my commitment to integrating AI with human needs.
